Job history

I added my most relevant jobs. For a full history, check out my LinkedIn profile.

  • Blockchain.com is one of the leading crypto companies out there. My focus there was on the exchange web app.

    My first challenge here was to work on a complicated codebase by myself. I was tasked to change the trade order input component so I reverse engineered the app to figure out how it worked. Then changed piece by piece to preserve functionality, doing lots of manual tests.

    My second big contribution was a bigger feature called simple trade. I interacted a lot with product manager, backend developers and designer. Which increases a lot my domain knowledge.

    Once I gained confidence around the app I took a chance to refactor some parts. I code implemented a route code split that reduced a lot first page load time. Refactored out dead code. And refactored the whole app to typescript. All this prove to speed up the app.

    Even though I was part of the frontend developers team, I was the sole developer for a while. That made me the point of contact for exchange questions around the company. Which stayed the same until my last day there.

    Eventually we grew into a team of 4. Which caused me to change a bit my positions into support for my teammates. I conducted team meetings, scheduled regular releases and set up objectives aligned with company goals.

  • Xapo is a bitcoin friendly fintech company. I worked there for a little over 5 years.

    I joined as a web team leader when the company was still small and stayed there until it was about 250 people.

    While young the company had a legacy webapp with some technical issues. I was assigned that app. My first steps were to improve it's release process and how to test it. Along with a team of 2 remote devs we implemented great features like the first debit card program. I learned the importance of deadlines and suffered from some urgent priorities shifts.

    Later on the company grew and we started hiring. The team grew from 2 to 8 devs, all of them remote with different time zones. By then we could finally invest in a new modern web application. Xapo webapp (Sorry, discontinued now) is the biggest most challenging project I have worked on. It is a single page app built with React, redux and redux-saga. It uses a large set of techs which include: Docker, Firebase, AWS, Google analytics, sentry.io and many many more. It was not the technology but the amount of features and constant iteration which made it very complex.

    We had 2 weeks sprints with demos and releases at the end of each one. I participated in the definition of release and development processes.

    I ended up handling multiple projects with different needs, technology and audiences. From public legal pages aspects to internal tools for Xapo employees.

    I left the company because most web projects were being shut down. I have a great relationship with most of the company including everyone on my team, my bosses and the CEO.

    As a manager I tried to promote collaboration and allow mistakes to be made. Every standard was up for debate, once settled it went on stone for a while. Team members were empowered to propose a change for what they didn't like. I pushed some company wide tech exchanges and some team wide ones like Fridays "tech talk"

    Xapo was a great place for me to exchange experiences due to the diversity of roles people had in the company. I learned from debit and credit cards, compliance, design, trading, working with third parties, law and banking institutions.

  • Kinetica Solutions was a software consulting firm.

    I worked there for a little more than 4 years.

    Kinetica was where I formed myself as a senior developer. I was lucky to work with great developers. Company founders were strong believers in quality and agile methodologies.

    There I started my transition from a backend developer to a frontend one. I got to work on multiple projects from classic custom CRUDs for internal users on a retail company, to news sites widgets with millions of views per day.

    In this company I moved from mentored to mentor. I did frontend, I did backend, I did DBA, I did operations. Passed through a lot of tech and people, who molded my way of thinking.
